# OPEN_SCIENCE_DISCLOSURE_POLICY.md

## 1. PURPOSE
This policy defines the rules and boundaries governing Open Science disclosures within the DeSci repository. Its objective is to promote transparency, reproducibility, and public trust while preserving patient safety, data protection, intellectual integrity, and regulatory compliance.

Open science is a governed obligation, not unrestricted release.

## 2. SCOPE
This policy applies to:
- Research findings and publications
- Methodological disclosures
- Aggregate performance metrics
- Governance-relevant research outputs
- Public-facing scientific communications

It does not mandate disclosure of protected or restricted materials.

## 3. DISCLOSURE PRINCIPLES
All open science disclosures must adhere to:
- Safety-first prioritization
- Lawful and ethical data use
- Scientific accuracy and completeness
- Contextual integrity
- Non-misleading representation

Transparency must never compromise individuals or systems.

## 4. DISCLOSABLE MATERIALS
Subject to review and approval, the following may be disclosed:
- Research objectives and hypotheses
- Study designs and methodologies
- Aggregate and de-identified results
- Model performance summaries
- Limitations and uncertainty statements

Disclosure must be proportional and justified.

## 5. RESTRICTED MATERIALS
The following are prohibited from open disclosure:
- Identifiable or re-identifiable personal data
- Protected health information
- Raw clinical datasets
- Security-sensitive system details
- Proprietary algorithms where disclosure creates risk
- Unvalidated or misleading interim results

Exceptions require formal approval and documentation.

## 6. REVIEW AND APPROVAL PROCESS
All disclosures require:
- Scientific validity review
- Ethics and data governance approval
- Compliance verification
- Documentation of scope and audience

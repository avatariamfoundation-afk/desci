# DAO_ROLE_ELIGIBILITY_AND_RESTRICTIONS.md

## 1. Purpose
This document defines the eligibility criteria, appointment requirements, and operational restrictions for all roles within the NeuroGrid DAO. Its purpose is to ensure that governance authority is exercised only by qualified, accountable, and ethically aligned participants in a clinical-grade system.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all formal DAO roles, including:
- Core Governance Members
- Committee members and chairs
- Safety Council members
- Audit and oversight roles
- Delegated authorities
- Temporary or emergency role holders

Informal contributors and observers are excluded except where explicitly referenced.

## 3. Governance Principles
Role assignment is governed by:
- Competence over popularity
- Accountability over anonymity
- Safety over decentralization
- Independence over financial influence

No role exists without defined responsibility and constraint.

## 4. General Eligibility Requirements
All role candidates must:
- Be legally eligible to participate
- Demonstrate relevant domain competence
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Agree to governance, ethics, and compliance obligations
- Accept auditability and role-based accountability

Failure to meet these requirements disqualifies eligibility.

## 5. Role Categories

### 5.1 Core Governance Members
Eligibility requires:
- Demonstrated long-term contribution or stewardship
- Proven understanding of NeuroGrid governance and regulatory context
- Approval through formal DAO vote

Restrictions:
- Subject to enhanced conflict-of-interest scrutiny
- May not unilaterally override safety or compliance constraints

### 5.2 Committee Members
Eligibility requires:
- Domain expertise relevant to committee mandate
- Appointment or confirmation per committee charter

Restrictions:
- Authority limited to committee scope
- No cross-committee authority without explicit approval

### 5.3 Safety Council Members
Eligibility requires:
- Clinical, safety, or regulatory expertise
- Independence from commercial incentives

Restrictions:
- May act preemptively only under defined emergency powers
- Subject to mandatory post-action review

### 5.4 Audit and Oversight Roles
Eligibility requires:
- Demonstrated independence
- No direct operational control over audited systems

Restrictions:
- Prohibited from participating in decisions they audit
- Financial or operational conflicts are disqualifying

### 5.5 Delegated Authorities
Eligibility requires:
- Explicit delegation via DAO vote or charter
- Clearly defined scope and duration

Restrictions:
- No authority beyond delegated mandate
- Delegation is revocable at any time

## 6. Role Incompatibilities
The following role combinations are prohibited:
- Audit and operational control roles
- Safety oversight and model development roles
- Governance voting authority and undisclosed financial interest

Incompatibilities must be resolved before role activation.

## 7. Conflict of Interest Disclosure
All role holders must:
- Submit initial and periodic disclosures
- Update disclosures upon material change
- Recuse themselves where conflicts exist

Non-disclosure constitutes a governance violation.

## 8. Term Limits and Review
Roles may be:
- Term-limited
- Subject to periodic performance and compliance review
- Revoked for cause

Automatic renewal is prohibited unless explicitly defined.

## 9. Suspension and Removal
Roles may be suspended or revoked due to:
- Breach of governance rules
- Ethical violations
- Regulatory non-compliance
- Loss of eligibility criteria

Removal actions are recorded and auditable.

## 10. Emergency Role Assignment
During declared emergencies:
- Temporary roles may be assigned
- Scope and duration must be explicitly defined
- Post-emergency ratification is mandatory

Emergency roles expire automatically.

## 11. Enforcement
Violations of this policy may result in:
- Role suspension or removal
- Loss of voting privileges
- Referral to compliance or legal authorities

Enforcement actions are binding and recorded.

## 12. Regulatory Alignment
This policy aligns with:
- DAO governance best practices
- EU AI Act governance expectations
- Clinical governance standards
- ISO 37301 compliance management principles

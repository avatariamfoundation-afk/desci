# DAO_CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md
**Professional Conduct, Integrity & Behavioral Standards**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **mandatory code of conduct** governing all participants in NeuroGrid DAO activities.

Its objectives are to:
- Enforce ethical, professional, and lawful behavior
- Protect patient safety, scientific integrity, and public trust
- Prevent abuse of governance power
- Establish clear expectations and consequences

Conduct standards are treated as **governance safety controls**, not community norms.

---

## 2. Scope

This code applies to:
- DAO members and voters
- Proposal authors
- Committee and council members
- Delegates and representatives
- Contributors acting in governance capacity

It applies across **Core**, **MedIntel**, and **DeSci** governance interactions.

---

## 3. Core Conduct Principles

All participants must adhere to:

1. **Integrity** – Act honestly and transparently  
2. **Responsibility** – Prioritize patient safety and public interest  
3. **Respect** – Maintain professional, non-abusive discourse  
4. **Accountability** – Accept scrutiny and consequences  
5. **Lawfulness** – Comply with applicable regulations and policies  

---

## 4. Expected Behaviors

Participants must:
- Engage in good-faith deliberation
- Disclose relevant conflicts of interest
- Respect governance processes and outcomes
- Use evidence-based reasoning
- Protect confidential and sensitive information

Clinical, safety, and compliance concerns must always take precedence over personal or financial interests.

---

## 5. Prohibited Conduct

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:

- Harassment, intimidation, or coercion
- Governance manipulation or vote buying
- Misrepresentation of data or credentials
- Suppression of safety or ethics concerns
- Retaliation against whistleblowers
- Circumvention of quorum, timelocks, or audits
- Use of insider or non-public information for advantage

---

## 6. Conflicts of Interest

Participants must:
- Disclose material conflicts prior to participation
- Recuse themselves where required
- Avoid influence over decisions where conflicts exist

Failure to disclose conflicts constitutes a governance violation.

---

## 7. Communication Standards

All governance communications must be:
- Professional and factual
- Non-deceptive
- Free from threats or inflammatory language
- Appropriate for public audit

Private or off-chain decision-making intended to bypass governance is prohibited.

---

## 8. Enforcement Mechanisms

Violations may result in:
- Formal warnings
- Proposal invalidation
- Temporary suspension of voting rights
- Role removal
- Permanent DAO expulsion
- Referral to independent oversight or regulators

Severity determines enforcement level.

---

## 9. Reporting Violations

Violations may be reported through:
- Designated DAO reporting channels
- Independent oversight mechanisms
- Whistleblower-protected submissions

All reports are reviewed confidentially and without retaliation.

---

## 10. Due Process

Enforcement actions must include:
- Clear allegation description
- Evidence review
- Opportunity to respond
- Documented decision rationale
- Right to appeal where applicable

Summary outcomes are disclosed publicly.

---

## 11. Audit & Recordkeeping

All conduct actions must be:
- Logged
- Attributable
- Time-stamped
- Auditable

Records are immutable and retained per governance policy.

---

## 12. Regulatory Alignment

This code aligns with:
- ISO 37001 anti-bribery standards
- ISO 37000 governance guidance
- OECD ethical governance principles
- Medical and research ethics norms

---

## 13. Ethical Position

> **Governance without integrity is a liability, not a system.**

Professional conduct is non-negotiable in clinical-grade DAOs.

---

## 14. Binding Status

This document is:
- Mandatory for all DAO participants
- Enforced through governance mechanisms
- Subject to independent audit
- Amendable only via compliant DAO process

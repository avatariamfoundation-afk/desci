# DAO_SANCTIONS_AND_ENFORCEMENT.md  
**Accountability, Enforcement & Corrective Action Framework**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **sanctions and enforcement mechanisms** of the NeuroGrid DAO.

Its purpose is to:
- Preserve ethical governance
- Deter misconduct
- Protect patients, data, and infrastructure
- Ensure regulatory defensibility
- Maintain public trust

Sanctions exist to **protect the system**, not to punish individuals.

---

## 2. Governance Principle

> **Authority without accountability is systemic risk.**

Enforcement must be:
- Proportionate
- Transparent
- Reviewable
- Consistent

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:
- DAO members
- Delegates
- Contributors
- Committee members
- Emergency authority holders
- External partners under DAO jurisdiction

---

## 4. Sanctionable Conduct

Sanctions may be applied for:
- Policy violations
- Ethical breaches
- Conflict of interest violations
- Negligence or misconduct
- Abuse of authority
- Data misuse
- Security compromise
- Regulatory risk creation
- DAO process manipulation

Intent is not required for enforcement.

---

## 5. Sanction Levels

### Level 1 – Advisory Actions
- Formal warning
- Required remediation plan
- Increased monitoring

### Level 2 – Restrictive Actions
- Temporary role suspension
- Voting rights suspension
- Access limitation
- Committee removal

### Level 3 – Corrective Actions
- Permanent role removal
- Delegation revocation
- Funding access termination
- Public disclosure of breach

### Level 4 – Protective Actions
- Emergency suspension
- Smart contract restrictions
- Registry access revocation
- External authority notification (if required)

---

## 6. Enforcement Authority

| Action Type | Authority |
|-----------|----------|
| Level 1 | Committee or DAO |
| Level 2 | DAO vote |
| Level 3 | DAO supermajority |
| Level 4 | Emergency authority (time-limited) |

No single entity holds unlimited enforcement power.

---

## 7. Enforcement Process

### 7.1 Initiation
- Incident report
- Governance proposal
- Audit finding
- Ethics review trigger

### 7.2 Review
- Evidence assessment
- Risk evaluation
- Conflict screening

### 7.3 Decision
- DAO vote or authorized action
- Proportionality assessment
- Recorded justification

---

## 8. Emergency Enforcement

Emergency sanctions may be applied when:
- Patient safety is at risk
- System integrity is threatened
- Regulatory exposure is imminent

Emergency actions:
- Are temporary
- Must be reviewed within 7 days
- Require post-incident disclosure

---

## 9. Due Process & Rights

Subjects of enforcement are entitled to:
- Notice of allegations
- Opportunity to respond
- Evidence access (where safe)
- Appeal mechanisms

Due process may be temporarily limited in emergencies.

---

## 10. Appeals Mechanism

- Written appeal submission
- Independent review panel
- Governance vote for reversal

Appeals do not automatically suspend sanctions.

---

## 11. Sanction Transparency

All sanctions are:
- Publicly recorded
- Time-stamped
- Linked to policy breaches
- Archived permanently

Sensitive details may be redacted where justified.

---

## 12. Sanctions & Funding

Sanctioned entities may:
- Lose funding eligibility
- Have grants frozen
- Be excluded from future rounds

Funding sanctions require heightened review.

---

## 13. Smart Contract Enforcement

Where applicable:
- On-chain restrictions may be applied
- Access control updates executed
- Enforcement logs immutably stored

Manual overrides are prohibited without governance approval.

---

## 14. Non-Retaliation Clause

Retaliation against whistleblowers or reporters is prohibited and sanctionable.

---

## 15. Regulatory Escalation

When legally required:
- Authorities may be notified
- Cooperation is mandatory
- DAO records may be disclosed

---

## 16. Rehabilitation & Reinstatement

Reinstatement requires:
- Remediation completion
- Risk reassessment
- Governance approval

Certain violations permanently bar reinstatement.

---

## 17. Policy Amendments

Changes require:
- Governance proposal
- Supermajority approval
- Public disclosure

---

## 18. Binding Status

This policy is:
- Mandatory
- Enforceable
- Governance-critical

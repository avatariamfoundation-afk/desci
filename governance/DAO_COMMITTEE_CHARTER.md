
# DAO_COMMITTEE_CHARTERS.md

## 1. PURPOSE
This document defines the formal charters governing all standing and ad hoc committees operating under the DAO structure. Committees exist to ensure that governance, scientific, clinical, and compliance responsibilities are executed with expertise, accountability, and regulatory alignment.

Committees are instruments of structured governance, not parallel power centers.

## 2. SCOPE
These charters apply to all DAO-recognized committees involved in:
- Clinical safety and oversight
- AI model governance
- Data governance and ethics
- Regulatory compliance
- Research review and funding
- Emergency response

No committee may operate without an approved charter.

## 3. GOVERNANCE PRINCIPLES
All committees operate under the following principles:
- Independence of judgment
- Domain-specific expertise
- Accountability to DAO governance
- Transparency of mandate and decisions
- Alignment with safety and regulatory obligations

## 4. COMMITTEE FORMATION
Committees are formed through:
- Formal DAO approval
- Defined mandate and scope
- Documented membership criteria
- Declared authority boundaries

Informal or undocumented committees are prohibited.

## 5. STANDARD COMMITTEE TYPES

### A. Clinical Safety Committee
Mandate:
- Review clinical risk
- Evaluate adverse events
- Approve safety-critical thresholds
- Advise on clinician override policies

Authority Level: High (Safety-Critical)

### B. AI Governance Committee
Mandate:
- Review AI model lifecycle decisions
- Assess model changes and impacts
- Enforce transparency and explainability standards
- Oversee bias and performance surveillance

Authority Level: High (Model Control)

### C. Data Governance & Ethics Committee
Mandate:
- Oversee data consent and use
- Review secondary use proposals
- Enforce data minimization and purpose limitation
- Address ethical concerns and disputes

Authority Level: High (Data Stewardship)

### D. Regulatory & Compliance Committee
Mandate:
- Interpret regulatory obligations
- Review compliance documentation
- Coordinate audits and inspections
- Advise on cross-border regulatory risk

Authority Level: High (Regulatory Alignment)

### E. Research Review Committee
Mandate:
- Evaluate research proposals
- Approve data access for research
- Monitor research integrity
- Enforce publication and disclosure standards

Authority Level: Medium (Research Integrity)

### F. Emergency Response Committee
Mandate:
- Act during declared emergencies
- Authorize temporary controls
- Coordinate rapid response actions
- Trigger post-event review and ratification

Authority Level: Time-Bound (Emergency Only)

## 6. MEMBERSHIP REQUIREMENTS
Committee members must:
- Meet domain expertise criteria
- Disclose conflicts of interest
- Agree to fiduciary and ethical obligations
- Maintain independence from conflicted decisions

Conflicted members must recuse themselves.

## 7. APPOINTMENT AND TENURE
- Members are appointed through defined governance processes
- Term limits apply to prevent entrenchment
- Removal requires documented cause

## 8. DECISION-MAKING AUTHORITY
Committees may:
- Issue recommendations
- Approve or block actions within mandate
- Escalate unresolved risks to DAO governance

Committees may not override immutable constraints.

## 9. RECORDKEEPING
All committee actions must be:
- Documented
- Timestamped
- Stored immutably
- Auditable by oversight bodies

Undocumented decisions are invalid.

## 10. TRANSPARENCY
Committee charters, mandates, and high-level decisions must be publicly disclosed where lawful and appropriate.

## 11. INTERACTION WITH DAO VOTING
Committee findings may:
- Inform DAO votes
- Act as gating conditions for proposals
- Trigger higher voting thresholds

DAO votes may not ignore binding committee findings without justification.

## 12. REVIEW AND AMENDMENT
Committee charters are subject to:
- Periodic review
- Formal amendment procedures
- Oversight validation

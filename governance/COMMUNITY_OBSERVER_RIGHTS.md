# COMMUNITY_OBSERVER_RIGHTS.md

## 1. PURPOSE
This document defines the formal rights, boundaries, and protections afforded to Community Observers within the DeSci-governed ecosystem. Community Observers exist to enhance transparency, trust, and accountability without exerting control over safety-critical or regulated decisions.

Observation is a governance function, not an authority.

## 2. SCOPE
This policy applies to all individuals or entities granted Observer status across:
- DAO governance processes
- Research governance activities
- Policy development and amendment cycles
- Public disclosure mechanisms
- Oversight-visible system operations

Observer status does not confer membership or decision rights.

## 3. OBSERVER STATUS DEFINITION
Community Observers are:
- Non-voting participants
- Read-only stakeholders
- Independent of operational control
- Protected participants in transparency processes

Observer status may be granted to:
- Researchers
- Civil society representatives
- Patient advocates
- Independent technologists
- Public interest organizations

## 4. GRANTED RIGHTS
Community Observers have the right to:
- Access published governance records
- View proposals, rationales, and outcomes
- Observe committee mandates and charters
- Review non-restricted audit summaries
- Access public research and transparency dashboards

Rights are bounded by law and safety constraints.

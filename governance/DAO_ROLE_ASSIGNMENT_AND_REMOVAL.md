# DAO_ROLE_ASSIGNMENT_AND_REMOVAL.md  
**Authority Allocation, Accountability & Revocation Framework**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **formal process for assigning, managing, and removing roles** within the NeuroGrid DAO.

Its purpose is to ensure:
- Clear accountability
- Prevention of power concentration
- Regulatory defensibility
- Continuity of critical operations
- Ethical governance integrity

---

## 2. Governance Principle

> **Authority is delegated, not owned.  
All roles exist to serve the protocol, patients, and public interest.**

No role is permanent or immune to review.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:
- DAO members
- Core contributors
- Committee participants
- Emergency authority holders
- Any individual with delegated decision power

---

## 4. Role Categories

### 4.1 Governance Roles
- DAO Member
- Delegate
- Committee Member
- Proposal Reviewer

### 4.2 Operational Roles
- Core Maintainer
- Registry Steward
- Compliance Lead
- Ethics Officer

### 4.3 Technical Roles
- Smart Contract Maintainer
- AI Model Steward
- Infrastructure Operator

### 4.4 Emergency & Oversight Roles
- Emergency Authority Holder
- Audit Coordinator
- Incident Response Lead

---

## 5. Role Assignment Criteria

All role candidates must demonstrate:
- Relevant competence
- Ethical alignment
- Absence of unresolved conflicts of interest
- Capacity to fulfill duties
- Agreement to governance obligations

Certain roles require additional credential verification.

---

## 6. Assignment Authority

| Role Type | Assignment Method |
|---------|------------------|
| Standard Roles | DAO governance vote |
| Committee Roles | Committee + DAO ratification |
| Emergency Roles | DAO vote with time limits |
| Interim Roles | Temporary appointment with expiry |

No individual may self-assign a role.

---

## 7. Term Limits

- Default role term: **12 months**
- Emergency roles: **≤ 90 days**
- Renewable only via governance review
- Automatic expiration if not renewed

Term limits prevent entrenchment.

---

## 8. Role Responsibilities

Each role must have:
- Defined scope
- Explicit authority boundaries
- Clear deliverables
- Accountability metrics

Undefined authority is prohibited.

---

## 9. Role Acceptance Requirements

Before activation, role holders must:
- Acknowledge role duties
- Accept conflict-of-interest rules
- Agree to transparency obligations
- Submit disclosure statements

Failure voids assignment.

---

## 10. Performance Review

Roles are subject to:
- Periodic review
- Triggered review upon incidents
- Review at term expiration

Reviews may result in:
- Renewal
- Scope adjustment
- Suspension
- Removal

---

## 11. Grounds for Removal

A role holder may be removed for:
- Breach of policy
- Conflict of interest violations
- Negligence or misconduct
- Abuse of authority
- Inactivity or failure to perform
- Regulatory risk creation

Intent is not required for removal.

---

## 12. Removal Process

### 12.1 Standard Removal
1. Proposal submission
2. Evidence review
3. DAO vote
4. Execution

### 12.2 Emergency Removal
- Immediate suspension
- Temporary authority transfer
- Post-action review within 7 days

---

## 13. Voting Restrictions During Removal

- The subject of removal may not vote
- Delegated votes are suspended
- Quorum excludes conflicted parties

---

## 14. Appeals Process

Removed individuals may:
- Submit written appeal
- Request independent review
- Seek reinstatement via governance vote

Appeals do not automatically reinstate authority.

---

## 15. Role Transition & Continuity

Upon removal or expiration:
- Access rights are revoked
- Documentation is transferred
- Interim coverage is assigned if required

No role may remain vacant if safety-critical.

---

## 16. Transparency & Records

All role assignments and removals are:
- Publicly logged
- Time-stamped
- Linked to proposals
- Immutable

---

- DAO accountability standards
- Ethical risk mitigation

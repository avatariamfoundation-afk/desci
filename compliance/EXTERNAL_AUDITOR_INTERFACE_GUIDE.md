# EXTERNAL_AUDITOR_INTERFACE_GUIDE.md

## 1. PURPOSE
This guide defines the standardized interface, access boundaries, and procedural expectations for external auditors engaging with the DeSci repository. Its purpose is to enable effective, lawful, and minimally disruptive audits while preserving system security, data protection, and governance integrity.

Auditor access is facilitated, not delegated.

## 2. SCOPE
This guide applies to all external audit activities, including:
- Regulatory inspections
- Compliance audits
- Security and controls assessments
- Independent governance reviews
- Certification and assurance engagements

Both on-site and remote audits are covered.

## 3. AUDITOR ELIGIBILITY
External auditors must:
- Be formally appointed or authorized
- Demonstrate relevant domain competence
- Execute required confidentiality and data protection agreements
- Declare conflicts of interest

Unauthorized auditors are prohibited from access.

## 4. ACCESS PRINCIPLES
Auditor access shall be:
- Purpose-limited
- Time-bound
- Read-only unless explicitly authorized
- Logged and auditable

No standing or unrestricted access is permitted.

## 5. AUTHORIZED ACCESS DOMAINS
Auditors may be granted access to:
- Governance policies and records
- Compliance documentation
- Audit logs and access logs
- Model lifecycle documentation
- Risk assessments and incident reports
- Oversight and committee records

Direct access to raw clinical data requires explicit approval.

## 6. ACCESS PROVISIONING
Auditor access must:
- Use dedicated auditor identities
- Be role-scoped and least-privilege
- Include start and end timestamps
- Be approved by governance or compliance authority

Shared credentials are prohibited.

## 7. DATA PROTECTION CONTROLS
Auditors must:
- Access only de-identified or aggregated data where possible
- Comply with applicable data protection laws
- Avoid data extraction unless authorized
- Secure all accessed materials

Data misuse constitutes immediate termination of access.

## 8. LOGGING AND MONITORING
All auditor interactions must be:
- Logged in accordance with access logging standards
- Monitored for anomalous activity
- Retained for audit and oversight review

Auditor actions are fully auditable.

## 9. INTERACTION PROTOCOLS
Audit interactions shall:
- Be coordinated through designated audit liaisons
- Avoid disruption of live clinical systems
- Follow documented communication channels

Ad hoc access requests are prohibited.

## 10. FINDINGS AND REPORTING
Auditors shall:
- Document findings clearly and accurately
- Distinguish observations from non-compliance
- Provide evidence-based conclusions
- Submit reports through approved channels

Draft findings may be subject to factual verification.

## 11. RESPONSE AND REMEDIATION
Audit findings will:
- Be reviewed by governance and compliance bodies
- Trigger corrective action plans where required
- Be tracked to resolution
- Be subject to follow-up verification

Unresolved critical findings require escalation.

## 12. CONFIDENTIALITY AND DISCLOSURE
Auditors must:
- Maintain confidentiality of all accessed information
- Refrain from unauthorized disclosure
- Return or securely destroy materials post-engagement

Public disclosure requires explicit authorization.

## 13. TERMINATION OF ACCESS
Auditor access must be terminated:
- At engagement completion
- Upon breach of terms
- If scope or authorization expires

Residual access is prohibited.

## 14. RECORDKEEPING
All audit engagements must be:
- Documented
- Logged and versioned
- Retained for regulatory and oversight review

Missing records invalidate audit assurance.

## 15. REGULATORY ALIGNMENT
This guide aligns with:
- ISO 19011 audit guidelines
- ISO 27001 and ISO 27701 audit controls
- GDPR and LGPD accountability principles
- FDA and EU regulatory audit expectations
